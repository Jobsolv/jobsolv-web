If your resume is not formatted correctly or lacks the right terms, it gets filtered out.</p><p>Your mission is to craft a single document that satisfies both the software and the person on the other side. You have to present your technical skills and business impact in a way that is immediately obvious. A messy or confusing layout can cause an ATS to scramble your information, getting you disqualified.</p><p>To make sure your resume gets parsed correctly, stick to these guidelines:</p><ul><li><strong>Use a Clean Template:</strong> Forget fancy designs with columns, tables, or excessive graphics. You need room to break down complex projects, showcase your technical toolkit, and quantify your impact. Squeezing everything onto a single page often means cutting the very details that would impress a hiring manager.</p><p>Research shows that two page resumes can actually perform better. You need to provide context that shows <em>how</em> you used those skills to solve real business problems. This approach proves you have both technical ability and a solid understanding of business goals.</p><p>Instead of a generic list, try organizing your skills to demonstrate depth and context.</p><p><strong>Example Skills Section:</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Programming &amp; Databases:</strong> Python (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit learn), R, SQL (PostgreSQL, MySQL), NoSQL (MongoDB)</li><li><strong>Data Visualization &amp; BI Tools:</strong> Tableau, Power BI, Looker, Matplotlib</li><li><strong>Cloud &amp; Big Data:</strong> AWS (S3, Redshift), Azure, Spark, Hadoop</li></ul><p>Even better, weave these skills directly into the bullet points under your work experience. Stop thinking about <em>what you did</em> and start focusing on <em>why it mattered</em>. Every bullet point on your resume is a small sales pitch, and it should close with an achievement, not a responsibility.</p><p>A simple formula to get this right is: <strong>Action Verb + Project + Result</strong>.</p><p>Here‚Äôs a classic example from data analysts.</p><ul><li><strong>Before (Task):</strong> &quot;Wrote SQL queries to analyze customer data.&quot;</li><li><strong>After (Impact):</strong> &quot;Developed complex SQL queries to analyze customer behavior, identifying a key segment that led to a <strong>10% increase</strong> in targeted marketing campaign conversions.&quot;</li></ul><p>The second version is a story. The key is to be credible and prepared to explain your math.</p><p>Here are a few places to start digging:</p><ul><li><strong>Time Saved:</strong> Did you automate a report or streamline a data pipeline?
